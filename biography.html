The Westie originated in Scotland. They were officially recognized in 1907 at the Crufts dog show in England. The name of the West Highland White Terrier was chosen on the rugged appearance of the dog and the area they were founded in (Scotland). The Westie stands around 11 to 10 inches and is one of the most popular dogs in the terrier group. They are strong and tough for a little dog and were used for hunting foxes, badgers, otter, and other vermin. In fact, the Westie is so strong that when it’s stuck you can pull on their tail and they won’t feel it. This is because the Westie has a bone in its tail making it okay for you to pull on when the Westie is stuck.
