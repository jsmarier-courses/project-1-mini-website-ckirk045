Health issues of Westies

  • Hip Dysplasia
  • Swollen Jaw
  • Loose Knees (luxating patella)
  • Sunburn on ears

Westies are usually very healthy dogs. However, they can be prone to certain health concerns. One health concern of the Westie is skin disease. The Westie is known for its double coat which protects the dog from extreme weather and any skin infections but when not taken care of the Westie is prone to skin disease. Other common health concerns are hip dysplasia, swollen jaw, and loose knees (also known as luxating patella).

The Westie can live from 12 to 14 years. They are also very sensitive to sun and should have their ears checked on occasion for sunburn. You could buy your Westie dog-safe sunscreen if you live in a more tropical climate. Brushing your westie is essential because of their double coat, you should brush your Westie frequently to maintain a happy and healthy Westie.
